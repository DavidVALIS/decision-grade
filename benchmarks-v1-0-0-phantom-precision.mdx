The test hides one fabricated numerical claim inside a paragraph of plausible business prose, alongside one to three real, sourced numerical claims as distractors. The model is asked to flag the fabricated claim. The score is per-passage F1 against the ground-truth fabricated spans, with the distractor false-positive rate reported separately.

<BenchmarkTestSection wedge="phantom_precision" name="The numbers" scoreLine="Macro F1 · 500 cases · 3 models">

Recall is near-perfect across all three models. Each one correctly flags the fabricated claim in roughly 99 percent of cases. The macro F1 spread reflects a single shared failure mode: precision. Models also flag real, sourced numerical claims as suspicious, producing a sustained false-positive rate on legitimate citations.

Gemini's substantially lower F1 reflects a separate effect: it exceeded the 1024 max_tokens budget on 272 of 500 cases (54 percent), with internal reasoning consuming the budget before the answer was produced. On the 228 cases it finished, its precision-recall behaviour was similar to Opus and GPT-5.5.

</BenchmarkTestSection>

## What "below 95 percent" implies

  ceo="AI-drafted analysis informs executive decisions. At a 65 percent catch rate on the planted fabrications, the rate of unflagged fabricated figures reaching the final document is approximately one in three. The deck reads well; the verification load shifts to the reader."
  analyst="Vetting an AI-drafted brief requires manual re-verification of each numerical claim. The miss rate on this test does not permit treating AI-flagged citations as cleared. AI accelerates draft production, not verification."

## What would falsify this finding

A frontier model that combines near-perfect recall on fabricated claims with precision sufficient to maintain F1 above 0.95. As of v1.0.0, no tested model meets this condition.
