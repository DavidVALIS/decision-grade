## How this feed works

Every refresh is a git commit. The change history is public on the [repository](https://github.com/DavidVALIS/decision-grade/commits/main/data/) and can be diffed week over week.

The scanner is project-scoped to the AI verification frontier. The signals here are tilted toward that frontier specifically, not the broader AI ecosystem. The grounding chips on each item map to the scanner&apos;s confidence read: *Verified* is anchored to a confirmed source or measurement, *Analytical* is derived through reasoning from prior evidence, *Peripheral* is an early indicator that has not yet hardened.

## What this feed is not

A market scorecard. Vendors and platforms are not ranked, and the absence of a name in the watch list is not an endorsement.

A real-time stream. Updates run on a weekly clock, not on event firing. Use the briefing for direction-of-travel, not for breaking news.

A guarantee of completeness. The scanner is one analytical layer over publicly available evidence and the VALIS research base. Important signals can sit outside its instrumented horizon, and the Peripheral grounding tag is the closest this readout comes to flagging that uncertainty.

## How to contest a reading

Disagreements file as issues on the [decision-grade repository](https://github.com/DavidVALIS/decision-grade/issues), the same channel that holds disagreements on the doctrine itself. Watch-list tripwires are the most precise place to contest, because each one specifies a falsification condition that a careful reader can pressure on its own terms. Individual signals can also be challenged; each entry in the feed names its source so the underlying claim is reachable directly.
