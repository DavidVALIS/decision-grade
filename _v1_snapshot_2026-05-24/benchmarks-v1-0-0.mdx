500 questions on each of six tests. Three frontier models took the same exam. Each test asks a different question a careful analyst already asks in their head before signing off. The graphs below show what happened, with the 95 percent decision-grade bar drawn across each one.

<BenchmarkResultsGrid />

## The headline

The best AI in the world cleared the 95 percent bar on two of the six tests. The other four fell short. The hardest one, "notice what is missing from an analysis," had every model below 41 percent. The easiest one, "identify the load-bearing claim in an argument," had two models above 95.

There is no clean winner. GPT-5.5 leads four of six. Opus leads the other two, including the only test where AI is *almost* trustworthy as a citation-checker. Gemini sits in the middle on three tests and gets squeezed by token-budget cutoffs on the others. The numbers reward different models on different jobs.

## What sits behind each chart

Each per-test page walks through the same three things in plain language. What the test asks. What the model did, in numbers your operations team can act on. What "below 95 percent" looks like for a CEO, an analyst, and an operator running agents through the night. Pick any of the six above to open the deep read.
